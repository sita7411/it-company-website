FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

UI/UX design is about creating digital products that are both easy to use and visually appealing. UI (User Interface) deals with how a product looks — its buttons, colors, and layout. UX (User Experience) focuses on how it works — how smoothly users can navigate and complete tasks. Together, UI and UX make websites and apps attractive, functional, and user-friendly.

UI/UX designers often face challenges such as understanding user behavior, balancing aesthetics with functionality, and meeting business goals while keeping the user experience intuitive. Designing for different devices and screen sizes, maintaining consistency, and handling user feedback effectively can also be tricky. Another major challenge is ensuring accessibility for all users, including those with disabilities, without compromising the visual appeal. Additionally, staying updated with evolving design trends and tools is essential but demanding.

Our UI/UX design process begins with understanding the user and business needs through research. We then create wireframes and user flows to plan the structure, followed by designing high-fidelity UI in tools like Figma. After building interactive prototypes, we test with real users, gather feedback, and refine the design to ensure it's both functional and visually appealing.

The cost of UI/UX design depends on the project scope, complexity, and number of screens or pages. Simple designs for landing pages or small apps may cost less, while large platforms with custom flows, prototyping, and testing require more time and investment. Pricing can range from ₹10,000 to ₹1,00,000+, depending on features, timelines, and revisions.

We design UI/UX for a wide range of platforms including web apps, mobile apps (iOS & Android), desktop software, eCommerce platforms, admin dashboards, landing pages, and SaaS products. Our designs are responsive, user-friendly, and tailored for each device type.

The timeline depends on the project’s size and complexity. A basic UI/UX design can take 1–2 weeks, while larger apps or platforms may take 3–6 weeks or more, including research, wireframing, visual design, and testing.
